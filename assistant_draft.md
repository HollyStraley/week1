## Industry Problem: Master Scheduling in Manufacturing

Master Scheduling at a large manufacturing company is an incredibly complex process that becomes even more challenging when managed entirely through paper-based, manual methods. Without digital tools, schedulers must rely on handwritten records and manual calculations to track inventory, capacity, and production demands across potentially hundreds of products and work centers, leaving significant room for human error. When disruptions occur — such as a machine breakdown or a late material delivery — updating the schedule by hand is a slow and labor-intensive process that can cause cascading delays across the entire production floor. The lack of real-time visibility and the inability to quickly run alternative scenarios makes it extremely difficult to make informed decisions and respond to change in an agile, timely manner.

## How Generative AI Could Help

- **Automated schedule generation:** AI could analyze demand forecasts, inventory levels, and capacity data to automatically generate and update master schedules, eliminating tedious manual calculations.
- **Scenario planning and what-if analysis:** AI could quickly model the impact of disruptions (e.g., a late supplier delivery or machine downtime) and suggest optimized schedule adjustments in real time.
- **Natural language reporting and communication:** AI could translate complex scheduling data into plain-language summaries for stakeholders, making it easier to communicate production plans across departments without requiring everyone to interpret raw data.
